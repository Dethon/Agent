**Run date:** 2026-07-22
**Branch:** `noise`
**Spec:** [`docs/superpowers/specs/2026-07-22-stt-enhancement-eval-design.md`](../../../docs/superpowers/specs/2026-07-22-stt-enhancement-eval-design.md)

This is the deliverable of the phase-1 plan: an offline WER sweep asking whether any speech
enhancement earns hub-integration work. The report body (tables + decision block + caveats) is
the verbatim output of the `report` stage; everything above and below it is the annotation the
spec's Task 12 calls for.

## Run metadata

### Corpus (`mix` stage → `runs/round1/manifest.jsonl`)

- **165 utterances** = 15 enrollment takes × 11 conditions (clean + {speech, music} × {+15, +10,
  +5, 0, −5} dB).
- **Speakers / takes:** Dethon (takes 1–10) and Tradaly (takes 1–5) — the two real household
  voices, pulled from the production Pi's enrollment `enroll-*.wav`. 2 speakers, 15 takes.
- **Exclusions:** **0.** Validation drops any take whose clean-audio WER exceeds 0.30; the worst
  survivor was Dethon-10 at 0.25, so all 15 takes are included.
- **Interference sources:** speech = OpenSLR **SLR73** Peruvian Spanish (`es_pe_female` +
  `es_pe_male`) — a *competing speaker*; music = OpenSLR **MUSAN** music.
- **Mixing:** synthetic *additive* mixing at 16 kHz mono, target held at unit level and the bed
  scaled to a fixed per-file SNR. **No room acoustics, no reverberation, no real playback path.**

### Enhancement conditions (`process` stage → processors)

| Condition | What it is | Type |
|---|---|---|
| `raw` | Unprocessed mixture | baseline |
| `gtcrn` | Official streaming ONNX `gtcrn_simple.onnx` (`Xiaobin-Rong/gtcrn`, 16 kHz) | single-channel denoiser |
| `dfn3` | DeepFilterNet3 via `deepfilternet==0.5.6` (48 kHz round-trip; `torch==2.1.2+cpu` pin) | single-channel denoiser |
| `tse` | Target-speaker extraction — WeSep demo checkpoint `bsrnn_ecapa_vox1` (BSRNN band-split separator + frozen ECAPA-TDNN speaker encoder; VoxCeleb1 / **English-trained**; 16 kHz) | target-speaker extraction |

**TSE checkpoint provenance & why this one.** The Task 10 spike settled on the **WeSep demo
checkpoint (fallback Level 1)** — the top of the sanctioned fallback chain — because it cleared the
acceptance bar decisively and the lower fallbacks (USEF-TSE, ClearerVoice-MossFormer2) were never
reached. Signal-level acceptance on every `speech-snr+00` mix: **SI-SDR +17.55 dB mean *gain* to the
target** (min +12.13) with the competing speaker driven to **−29.35 dB mean** residual. Enrollment is
**no-leakage**: the speaker's *other* takes are concatenated and the take under test is always
excluded, so TSE never conditions on the utterance it is scored against. TSE runs as a persistent
worker in an isolated, gitignored `data/models/tse-env` (rebuildable from
`stt_eval/conditions/tse_env_setup.sh`), so it never perturbs the project venv's `torch==2.1.2+cpu`
pin that DFN3 depends on.

### Backends (`transcribe` stage)

| Backend | Identity | Role |
|---|---|---|
| `medium` | faster-whisper **1.2.1**, `medium` model, int8, **CPU**, `language=es`, `beam_size=5` (ctranslate2 4.8.1) | **Previews the planned Lemonade / whisper-medium migration target.** |
| `wyoming` | Repo's patched `jackbot-wyoming-whisper` image — Whisper **base** + Silero VAD (`--vad-filter --vad-threshold 0.6`), int8, `beam-size 5`, Spanish initial-prompt; emits the confidence `score` the hub gate reads | **Prod parity today.** |

- **Metrics:** `jiwer==4.0.0` WER/CER. Normalization = casefold + strip punctuation + collapse
  whitespace, **diacritics kept** (`stt_eval/textnorm.py`).
- **Decision rule** (`stt_eval/report_stage.py`): a model PASSES iff it cuts WER **≥ 20 % relative**
  in the low-SNR **speech**-interference cells (SNR ≤ 5 dB → the +5 / 0 / −5 dB points) **AND** does
  not regress clean WER by more than 0.02 absolute (`clean ΔWER ≤ 0.02`).

## Conclusions

### PASS / FAIL by backend

| Backend | dfn3 | gtcrn | **tse** |
|---|---|---|---|
| medium (whisper-medium, migration target) | FAIL (−4.1 % rel, clean +0.010) | FAIL (−6.1 % rel, clean +0.005) | **PASS (+80.5 % rel, clean +0.000)** |
| wyoming (whisper-base, prod today) | FAIL (+0.1 % rel, clean +0.034) | FAIL (−0.7 % rel, clean +0.001) | **PASS (+57.6 % rel, clean −0.004)** |

- **TSE passes both backends decisively and is the only condition that passes either.** On `medium`
  it collapses low-SNR speech WER from 92.4 % (raw) to 18.0 % (+80.5 % relative) while leaving clean
  WER *untouched* (Δ +0.000). On prod-parity `wyoming` it still cuts low-SNR speech WER 97.6 % → 41.4 %
  (+57.6 % relative) with clean WER slightly *better* (Δ −0.004). This is exactly the case the spec
  predicted: a competing *speaker* is a target-selection problem, and only TSE — conditioned on the
  enrolled voice — can pick the target out; the denoisers have no notion of "which speaker."
- **Both denoisers FAIL both backends, and actively hurt.** DFN3 and GTCRN *raise* WER in the low-SNR
  speech cells relative to raw (negative relative improvement on `medium`) and DFN3 also regresses
  clean WER past the gate on `wyoming` (+0.034). They over-suppress: on `medium` the denoisers empty
  out the transcript entirely on many low-SNR clips (dfn3 produced 20 empty hyps, gtcrn 12, vs 4 for
  raw). Single-channel denoising is the wrong tool for a co-channel speaker and should not advance.

### What this means for the spec's step-2 gate

The spec's decision rule is explicit that a PASS earns **only hub-integration *consideration*, and
that consideration itself gates on the phase-2 field corpus before any production wiring**. So the
outcome of round 1 is:

> **TSE is the sole surviving candidate. It advances to phase 2 (field recording with the real TV in
> fran-office), not to production.** No hub or STT-path code changes are warranted on the strength of
> this synthetic sweep alone. The denoisers are eliminated and should not be carried into phase 2.

Round 1 has done its job: it turned "should we build enhancement into the STT path?" into a single,
sharp phase-2 question — *does the WeSep TSE gain survive real room acoustics, real TV audio, and
Castilian speech, when driven from real far-field mic captures rather than synthetic additive mixes?*

### Honest caveats (read before over-trusting the numbers)

1. **English/VoxCeleb-trained TSE on Castilian.** The `bsrnn_ecapa_vox1` speaker encoder is trained
   on English VoxCeleb. Speaker extraction keys on voice *identity* not phonetics, so this is expected
   to transfer — and the WER drop bears that out — but a Spanish-specific degradation cannot be ruled
   out from 2 speakers. Phase 2 is where this gets stress-tested.
2. **Synthetic additive mixing ≠ the real problem.** These mixes have no room impulse response, no
   reverberation, and no acoustic echo path. The production complaint is a *TV across the room*; the
   real captures are far-field and reverberant. TSE's synthetic gain is an upper-bound optimistic
   estimate until phase 2 confirms it on field recordings.
3. **Only 2 speakers, 15 takes, scripted phrases.** Small corpus. Per-cell means are 15 utterances.
   Treat the tables as directional, not precise; the ±20 % gate is wide enough to absorb this, and
   TSE clears it with huge margin, but the denoiser near-misses are within noise.
4. **Digits-vs-words normalization noise.** References are scripted; whisper writes "10" where the
   reference says "diez" (and vice-versa), which `textnorm` does not reconcile. This inflates *every*
   condition's WER roughly equally (visible in the TSE hyps too), so it does not bias the *relative*
   comparison — but it means absolute WER floors are pessimistic.
5. **whisper-base has a high clean WER floor.** The `wyoming` (base) backend transcribes *clean* audio
   at 20.7 % WER — versus 4.2 % for `medium`. Its low-SNR numbers sit near the ceiling for every
   condition, compressing the dynamic range and making its decision numbers noisier than `medium`'s.
   Trust `medium` as the quality signal; `wyoming` shows the migration is worth it on its own (and
   that TSE helps even the weak base model). This alone is evidence for the planned Lemonade /
   whisper-medium migration.
6. **Low-SNR *music* rows are contaminated by whisper hallucination.** At music SNR ≤ 0 dB the raw
   transcripts degrade into fluent off-topic Spanish rather than errors on the target — YouTube-style
   boilerplate ("Gracias por ver el video", "¡SUSCRIBITE!"), repetition loops ("No te
   preocupes. No te preocupes…", "conmigo conmigo conmigo"), and transcription of the *music's own*
   lyrics ("TAKE ANOTHER STEP…"). This is a Whisper artifact on near-unintelligible audio, not a
   property of any enhancement. It inflates the music-column WER for all conditions and is one reason
   TSE helps *less* on music (a music bed has no speaker identity to null) — but music is the *easier*
   real-world case (the spec's driver is competing TV *speech*), so this does not change the verdict.

### Sanity checks performed (Task 12 Step 2)

- **Raw WER degrades monotonically with SNR** for all four backend×interference combinations
  (medium/speech 19→44→85→93→100; medium/music 4→6→18→54→74; wyoming both likewise), and speech
  interference is worse than music at equal SNR (e.g. medium +5 dB: speech 84.6 % vs music 18.2 %) —
  exactly as expected for a competing-speaker vs. noise-bed contrast. Raw clean WER is 4.2 % (medium),
  well under the 15 % "something upstream is wrong" tripwire.
- **Decision numbers independently recomputed** from `per_utterance.csv` reproduce the report block
  exactly (medium tse +80.5 % / Δ+0.000 PASS; wyoming tse +57.6 % / Δ−0.004 PASS; all denoisers FAIL).
- **Spot-read of 15 `speech snr-05` and 15 `speech snr+00` rows (raw vs tse, medium):** in the raw
  hyps the model locks onto the *interfering* speaker and returns fluent, on-topic-for-the-interferer
  Spanish (e.g. Dethon-t1 raw = "Edgar, quisiera hacer un depósito a la cuenta de mi primo"; Tradaly-t4
  raw = "Las salas IMAX tienen un costo de 110 soles") — none of the target words. TSE recovers the
  target phrase on the large majority (Dethon-t6/t9, Tradaly-t3/t5 → WER 0.00; most others ≤ 0.13 at
  +0 dB), with a few residual failures at −5 dB (Dethon-t1/t2/t4) consistent with the 31.9 % tse
  speech-−5 mean. The interferer words present in `raw` are absent from `tse`. Confirmed.
